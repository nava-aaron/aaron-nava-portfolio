THE BUILDS
PHASE 1: THE BUILDS — Circus (2017-2019: Building from Zero)

Building
Circus

Time: 2 Years Scale: 6 to 100+ People Rev: $5M to $30M+

The Situation

I met the CEO of Circus and something clicked. He had a vision I believed in. We built something amazing from scratch in the US.

Circus had offices in Mexico and Argentina. But the US? That was blank. Six people. Small accounts. We needed to prove to major brands that we could deliver at scale.

The Build

I inherited Spotify accounts in the US and saw a bigger opportunity. I grew those relationships into a Social AOR partnership. We positioned ourselves to lead the launch of Viva Latino, one of Spotify's biggest platform initiatives, and that became the blueprint for how we approached account expansion—finding gaps in existing relationships and filling them with strategic capabilities.

Then California Lottery. A formal government RFP. Six months. When we won, it changed everything. A five-year contract. Forecasting certainty. This win proved we could deliver at scale.

Startup mindset + operational rigor + relentless focus on client outcomes = sustainable growth. Culture compounds. Process matters.
PHASE 2: THE BUILDS — Monks (2019-2025: Integrating at Scale)

Integrating
Monks

Time: 4-5 Years Scale: 150 to 1,200 People Rev: $25M to $115M+

The Situation

S4 Capital acquired Circus. I'm integrating five core agencies, with 28 more coming. Each one with their own founders. Their own operating model. The capability was fragmented.

My mandate: unify the narrative. Create one go-to-market. Make sure that when you pitch a client, you're presenting one coherent capability.

The Execution

Listen First: I didn't force anything. I became the liaison. I asked questions. I learned what they knew about their clients.

Show the Math: After a year, the resistance broke because the data showed them: Better qualification = fewer pitches, higher close rate. Focus on bigger opportunities = bigger deals. Our win rate climbed to 85%.

For roughly three years, I was personally anchoring 22-25 major pursuits annually. The social-first capability became the second highest revenue for the entire $1B+ organization.

At enterprise scale, the problem isn't usually execution. It's alignment. Get smart leaders aligned on how you win, and the organization moves in one direction.

Some people manage the business. Some people build it. I've always been more interested in the architecture.

Four builds. Each one bigger, harder, and more complex than the last. Conill taught me how to launch a new capability from scratch inside an existing organization. How to find the white space, build the case, and turn a blank mandate into a revenue line. Joe Agency taught me the P&L the hard way, which is the only way it actually sticks. Circus was the proof of concept. Six people who grew into a focused, competitive agency that could walk into any room and win. Monks was the global enterprise test. The one where the stakes are real, the teams span continents, and the margin for error gets very small, very fast.

Here's what I've learned about building teams that most people figure out too late: the goal was never to be needed. The goal was always to make yourself unnecessary. Build the right people, give them the right systems, and get out of their way. The ones who figure that out early build things that last. The ones who don't become the bottleneck.

I've learned that profit is the outcome, not the objective. But you still have to go get it. I've always been drawn to the hunt, finding the opportunity before it's obvious, making the case before anyone asked, turning a conversation into a commitment. 85% of the pitches we ran, we won. That doesn't happen by accident. It happens because you build the kind of organization that knows how to grow, and then you go out and prove it every single day.

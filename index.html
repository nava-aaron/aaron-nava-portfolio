PHASE 1: THE BUILDS — Circus (2017-2019: Building from Zero)

Building
Circus

Time: 2 Years Scale: 0 to 0+ People Rev: $0M to $0M+

The Situation

The US operation was six people and small accounts. The opportunity was wide open. I didn't just believe in the vision. I saw exactly how to build it.

The Build

It started with Spotify. I inherited the account and immediately saw what it could become. Grew it from a single relationship into a full Social AOR partnership. When the opportunity came to lead the launch of Viva Latino, we were ready. Not because we got lucky. Because we'd spent months positioning ourselves as the only logical choice.

That became the playbook. Cultural fluency as a competitive advantage. Find the gap in an existing relationship, bring multicultural relevance and creative depth, expand from there. And when there's no relationship yet, go find the room, make the case, and earn the seat.

Then came California Lottery. A formal government RFP. Six months competing against agencies twice our size. When we won, it proved that a six-person US office could walk into any room and compete at any level.

Two years. 6 people to 100. $5M to $30M+. Ready for acquisition.
PHASE 2 — Monks (2019–2025: Integrating at Scale)

Integrating
Monks

Time: 4-5 Years Scale: 0 to 0 People Rev: $0M to $0M+

The Situation

Thirty-three agency founders. Each with their own identity, their own clients, their own way of winning. When S4 Capital began integrating agencies, I was inside that growth, building the social-first capability from the ground up across a fragmented, multi-region organization. The mandate: one go-to-market. One coherent capability. One way to win.

The Build

I started by listening. Not presenting. Not forcing. Trust came before alignment, it had to. The resistance was real. Founders protect what they built. But after a year, the data broke through what conversation couldn't. Better qualification, fewer pitches, higher close rates. The win rate climbed to 0%. The math became the argument.

Then came the global build. NAMER. LATAM. APAC. EMEA. Not from a distance, from the ground up. First hires, first markets, leadership teams built region by region. Every market started the same way: the right people and a clear system for how we win.

For three consecutive years I personally anchored 22 to 25 major global pursuits annually. $35M to $115M. Second highest revenue driver in the organization. 1,200 people at peak. You can't manage your way to that number. You need the pirate ship, fast, fearless, high performers who don't wait for permission. But you also need the structure that makes it repeatable. Most organizations pick one. We refused to. That tension is what drove us.

Some people manage the business. Some people build it. I've always been more interested in the architecture.

Four builds. Each one bigger, harder, and more complex than the last. Conill taught me how to find the white space and turn a blank mandate into a revenue line. Joe Agency taught me the P&L the hard way. Circus was the proof of concept. Monks was the global enterprise test, where the stakes are real, the teams span continents, and the margin for error gets very small, very fast.

The goal was never to be needed. It was always to make yourself unnecessary. Build the right people, give them the right systems, and get out of their way. That's what makes things last.

Profit is the outcome, not the objective. But you still have to go get it. I've always been drawn to the hunt, finding the opportunity before it's obvious, turning a conversation into a commitment. 85% of the pitches we ran, we won. That doesn't happen by accident.

Neither does the cultural edge. It's not a targeting parameter for me, it's how I think. I'm bilingual, bicultural, and I've built campaigns for Hispanic audiences at the highest level — Toyota, Dodge, Spotify, California Lottery. I don don't need a brief to understand the market. I am the market. And I've learned to build the tools that move it.

I've been deep in AI as a builder, not an observer. I hold an MIT certification in AI and Business Strategy, but more than credentials, I have a point of view: the pure specialist is shrinking. The next generation of high performers will direct, manage, and scale AI agents. That shift is already underway. I'm already building for it.

The machine that runs without you. That's always been the goal.
